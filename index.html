The problem we're solving

Gaps in American West's pricing operations today

The current spreadsheet-and-manual-process approach has served the business, but it was never designed for governed, auditable pricing at scale. These are the critical gaps a modern tariff and pricing platform directly addresses.

No governance

Discount and margin rules live in people's heads

Reps apply discounts without systematic approval routing. Margin floors and minimum charges are enforced inconsistently — revenue leakage is invisible until it's too late.

Spreadsheets

Tariff changes are fragile and untraceable

Rate updates happen in Excel workbooks — including 28 per-origin rate tabs (b2b_*, wgi_*, etc.) with no version history, no side-by-side diff, and no record of who changed what or when a rate became effective.

No provenance

Quotes lack a full audit trail

When a customer disputes a rate, there is no single record showing which tariff version, fuel surcharge, and discount produced the quoted price.

Slow

Every quote is built from scratch

Sales reps manually look up tariffs, fuel tables, B2B lane tables, home-delivery tiers, and per-service-type customer discounts — re-keying data that should resolve automatically from master records.

Disconnected

No customer self-service channel

Customers cannot request quotes, track shipments, or manage their address book online — every interaction routes through a sales rep or operations contact.

Blind spots

Sales pipeline visibility is limited

Quote aging, win/loss tracking, and rep performance live outside the pricing system — managers lack a unified view of opportunities tied to actual quoted rates.

Proposed architecture

Built to grow with you. Never to lock you in.

Every layer in the platform is designed as a pluggable component. Phase 1a (MVP) delivers the governed quote workflow across 13 features and five domain modules — no tariff version diff, quote comparison, or operations dashboard yet. Phase 1b restores those capabilities and completes internal ops; Phases 2 and 3 add portal and sales — all on the same hub-first architecture.

MVP scope (Phase 1a): Log in → price a shipment → govern the quote → produce a PDF. Two hubs active (Market Data, Document). Tariff CRUD, origin-station adjustment grid, consolidated rate table matrix, configurator, and override hierarchy; customer master with negotiated discounts by service level; plain quote list; full provenance. Commercial lanes, residential delivery tiers, TMS handoff mapping, agreement template, and reference-data UI graduate in Phase 1b.

Pricing configuration

Ten admin screens replace manual rate maintenance

Today’s pricing rules live across dozens of spreadsheet tabs. The platform consolidates them into ten governed configuration areas — many rate tables share one editor, while commercial lanes and residential delivery tiers stay separate because reps price them differently. Click any card for business scope and quote impact; open interactive demo screens from the Mockup index.

Click any configuration area for scope, ownership, and pricing impact ↓

Rating pipeline: ZIP → lane/tier → cube bracket → per-pound rate × weight → lane minimum → fuel on linehaul → insurance (1% DV, $25 min). Four service types run in parallel; any unresolved step returns Call-for-Quote.

    Proposed system integrations

    Connects to what American West already runs

    Integrations route through hub adapters. Phase 1a activates EIA fuel (engine config, not standalone UI) and PDF generation; Phase 1b adds version diff screens, DocuSign, TMS, and reference-data management; Phases 2–3 add portal tracking, identity, and CRM sync.

    All integrations use REST APIs where available, with scheduled file exchange as a fallback. Domain logic never calls vendors directly — every external touchpoint routes through the integration hub layer.

    EIA.gov Public APIMarket Data Hub
    Weekly diesel fuel price pull feeding the pricing engine's surcharge calculation. Historical rates retained with source reference on every quote.
    Phase 1a
    PDF GenerationDocument Hub
    Headless rendering adapter produces branded quote PDFs stored in object storage with retrievable URLs attached to quote records.
    Phase 1a
    DocuSignDocument Hub · E-signature
    Quote documents sent for e-signature directly from the quote workflow. Executed documents returned and archived to the quote record.
    Phase 1b
    TMS ExportOps Systems Hub
    Accepted quotes pushed to the transportation management system — normalized via configurable rate-key mapping (tariff/level codes, discount level, fuel index) before export.
    Phase 1b
    Freight TrackingOps Systems Hub
    Shipment status consumed for the customer portal tracker — active and completed shipments with milestone events and proof-of-delivery access.
    Phase 2
    SSO / MFAAPI Gateway · Identity
    Enterprise single sign-on and multi-factor authentication for portal customers — internal MFA ships in Phase 1b.
    Phase 2
    CRM SyncOps Systems Hub
    Opportunity and quote status synchronized with the sales CRM — pipeline stage updates driven by quote lifecycle events in the pricing system.
